Lets jump in with some astonishing facts about aluminium cans. Believe it our not aluminium cans come in a two-piece varieety, just like Dad's suit

  • We use over 80,000,000,000 aluminium soda cans evert year
  • The use of aluminium cans began in 1957
  • Aluminium can take between 200-500 years to degrade whilst in landfill
  • The largest collection of Coca-Cola soda cans belonged to Gary Feng of Canada with 11,308 variations of the Coca-Cola cans from 108 countries
  • Recycling aluminium takes 95% less energy than making aluminium from raw materials

And lets not forget about those plastic bottles with their pesky microplastics. There are though a load of cool things you can do with a bottle after you've finished your drink like a rainwater collector to measure rainfall or use the tops for mini cloches when starting off your flower seeds.

  • 90% of the cost of bottled water is the bottle itself
  • plastic was invested in 1907 by Leo Baekeland
  • 24 million litres of oil could produce 3 billion plastic bottles
  • But 80% of bottles never get recycled
  • Almost every piece of plastic ever made still exists today
